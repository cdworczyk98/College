Functional Testing

Functional testing involves checking that the whole application, or just a part of it, does what is supposed to do. This is achieved in three steps:

  1. The product is tested by the developer, as it is the developer who understands how the product is supposed to work.
  1. The product is given to the user/s, as they are the ones who know what it should do.
  1. The product is given to a third party together with the specification. If the third party does not know anything about the product, apart from what is written, the third party will not make assumptions, but more importantly, they will not worry about upsetting the developer, if there are any shortfalls with the product.

When testing the functionality of the application, we do not only test individual items such as reports, forms, queries, macros, etc. but, we also test how these items fit together. Just because a query runs, doesn't mean that the logic or code that uses it works. In our logic we might be using a field that isn't in the query. Individual items must be tested, and then, how they work together.
